Creating DEMS and 3D Visualizations

Processing Digital Elevation Models (DEMs) and generating effective 2D and 3D terrain visualizations for the Radiant Creek study area.

Final 3D scene of Radiant Creek using the processed DEM and shaded relief symbology. [cite: 63]

Project Overview

Digital Elevation Models (DEMs) are foundational datasets in GIS, representing terrain for analysis and visualization. This lab focused on essential DEM processing workflows within the Radiant Creek study area. The core goal was to gain proficiency in integrating multiple DEM files into a seamless raster, clipping it to a defined study area, and using the refined DEM to generate informative terrain visualizations like hillshades, shaded relief maps, and interactive 3D scenes.

My Role & Process

For this lab exercise, my responsibilities involved the complete workflow:

  • Combining segmented DEM data into a single seamless raster using the Mosaic to New Raster tool.
  • Creating a study area polygon feature class and digitizing an appropriate boundary, refining its shape using editing tools (Edit Vertices, Fillet) for smooth lines.
  • Clipping the mosaicked DEM to the study area polygon using the Clip Raster tool, ensuring correct parameters were used (clipping geometry, maintaining extent).
  • Generating a multidirectional hillshade with adjusted scaling and Z factor to enhance terrain features.
  • Creating a shaded relief raster, experimenting with illumination parameters and Z factor to best highlight Radiant Creek's features.
  • Converting the 2D map to a 3D local scene.
  • Setting the processed DEM as the ground elevation surface in the 3D scene.
  • Adjusting vertical exaggeration in the 3D scene for effective visualization.
  • Troubleshooting visualization parameters through iteration and research to achieve desired results.

Tools & Technologies

  • GIS Software: ArcGIS Pro
  • Key Tools: Mosaic To New Raster, Create Feature Class, Editor Toolbar (Edit Vertices, Fillet), Clip Raster, Hillshade, Shaded Relief, Map to Scene tools.
  • Data Sources: Provided segmented DEM files for Radiant Creek study area.

Results & Key Findings

All primary objectives of the lab were successfully met, demonstrating proficiency in DEM processing and visualization techniques. A seamless DEM was created, clipped accurately, and used to generate effective hillshade and shaded relief visualizations. Experimentation with illumination parameters and Z-factors proved crucial for accentuating specific terrain features within the Radiant Creek area. Finally, a useful 3D scene representation was created using the processed DEM as the elevation source. This exercise highlighted the importance of careful parameter selection in terrain visualization and provided practical skills in raster data management and 3D representation within ArcGIS Pro.
